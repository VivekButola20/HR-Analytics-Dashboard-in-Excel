# 🧑‍💼 HR Analytics Dashboard (Excel Project)

# About this Project--

### 📊 Project Overview
This project is an **HR Analytics Dashboard** created using **Microsoft Excel** to help HR managers and decision-makers understand employee data and workforce trends.  
The goal of this project is to analyze employee attrition (people leaving the company) and identify patterns related to **gender, age, education, job role, and department**.

## 🧠 Project Purpose
Every company wants to reduce employee turnover and improve retention.  
This dashboard helps HR teams answer questions like:
- How many employees have left the company?
- What is the **attrition rate**?
- Which **department or job role** has the highest attrition?
- Does **education level**, **age group**, or **marital status** affect attrition?
- How does **gender distribution** look across the organization?
- What is the **average employee age** and **job satisfaction**?

## 📈 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The dashboard includes the following main KPIs:

| KPI | Description |
|-----|--------------|
| **Total Employees** | Total number of employees in the company |
| **Attrition Count** | Number of employees who left the company |
| **Active Employees** | Employees currently working in the organization |
| **Attrition Rate** | Percentage of employees who left |
| **Average Age** | Average age of the total workforce |
| **Job Satisfaction Rating** | Overall employee satisfaction score |

## 📊 Dashboard Visuals and Insights

### 1. **Total Employees by Gender**
- Shows the number of male and female employees.  
- **Insight:** There are more male employees than female employees (approx. 60% male).

### 2. **Education-wise Attrition**
- Displays how education level impacts attrition.  
- **Insight:** Employees with Bachelor’s degrees have the highest attrition rate.

### 3. **Attrition by Job Role**
- Highlights which job roles see the most exits.  
- **Insight:** *Laboratory Technicians* and *Sales Executives* show the highest attrition.

### 4. **Attrition by Department**
- Breakdown of attrition across departments.  
- **Insight:** The *Sales* department shows the largest share of employee turnover.

### 5. **Attrition by Age Group**
- Helps identify which age group tends to leave most often.  
- **Insight:** Younger employees aged **25–34** have the highest attrition.

### 6. **Attrition by Marital Status**
- Compares attrition among single, married, and divorced employees.  
- **Insight:** Single employees are more likely to leave compared to married employees.

### 7. **Job Satisfaction Rating**
- Shows the average satisfaction rating (scale 1–5).  
- **Insight:** The overall job satisfaction is **2.7**, indicating a need for HR intervention.

---

## 💡 Business Insights Summary
- The **overall attrition rate is 16%**, which is moderate but requires attention.  
- The **Sales and R&D departments** have the highest attrition percentages.  
- **Younger and single employees** tend to leave more frequently, showing a need for engagement programs.  
- **Job satisfaction** is below average (2.7/5), suggesting that employees might feel undervalued or overworked.  
- **Bachelor’s degree holders** are the most affected group by attrition, possibly due to better job opportunities elsewhere.
